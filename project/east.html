TAWANG

Sangestar Lake

Sangestar Lake, also known as Madhuri Lake, is a pristine high-altitude lake in Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh, India. Located at 12,000 feet, it’s surrounded by snow-capped mountains and rhododendron forests. The lake formed after an earthquake in 1950. It’s a popular tourist spot for its stunning scenery and tranquility, accessible via a scenic drive from Tawang.

Bomdila

Bomdila is a peaceful hill station offering panoramic views of the Eastern Himalayas. The town is known for its Buddhist monasteries, including the Bomdila Monastery. Visitors can enjoy the scenic views of the Sela Pass and the nearby Dirang Valley.

Ziro Valley

Ziro Valley is home to the Apatani tribe, one of the most well-known indigenous tribes of Arunachal Pradesh. One of the most popular events in Ziro Valley is the Ziro Music Festival, a 4-day celebration of music, culture, and arts. The best time to visit Ziro Valley is from March to October.Hong Village is a quaint village that offers a deep insight into the Apatani culture.Kile Pakho is peak offers breathtaking views of the Ziro valley and is ideal for trekking enthusiasts looking to explore the natural beauty of the region.

Local Cuisine

Savor apong and bamboo shot fry. Arunachal Pradesh is known for it's spicy and tangy food . The cuisine is predominantly influenced by the indigenous tribal groups of the state, including the Monpa, Apatani, Nyishi, Mishing, and Adi communities,each with their distinct cooking styles and ingredients.Momos are steamed or fried dumplings filled with vegetables or meat (pork, chicken, or beef).Eeyap is a traditional rice beer consumed by many of the indigenous tribes of Arunachal Pradesh.

Shopping in Arunachal Pradesh

The state's rich cultural heritage, diverse indigenous tribes, and natural resources provide a variety of options for visitors to take home.The indigenous tribes of Arunachal Pradesh, such as the Monpa, Apatani, Nyishi, Adi, and Mishing, are known for their intricate handwoven textiles.Arunachal Pradesh is known for its organic products, including wild honey from the region's diverse flora. Herbal oils, medicinal plants, and herbal teas are also popular items for health-conscious buyers.

IMPHAL

Shree Govindjee Temple

Located in Imphal, this temple is dedicated to Lord Krishna and is an important pilgrimage site for followers of Vaishnavism. The temple was built during the reign of King Bhagyachandra, who is credited with promoting the Vaishnavite faith in Manipur in the 18th century.

Keibul Lamjao

Keibul Lamjao is famous for the phumdis (floating islands) that form the surface of Loktak Lake.The park is the only place in the world where the Sangai deer (also known as the Manipur brow-antlered deer) is found.Boating on Loktak Lake is a popular activity, offering visitors the chance to see the floating islands up close and catch glimpses of the wildlife.

Kangla Ford

The fort holds immense historical significance as the ancient seat of the kings of Manipur and is an important symbol of the state's rich heritage, traditions, and royal past. The word "Kangla" means "dry" or "without water" in the Meitei language, and the fort is closely associated with the history of the Meitei dynasty. The best time to visit Kangla Fort is during the winter months (October to March), when the weather is pleasant, and the fort is more accessible for tourists.

Local Cuisine

Eromba is one of the most iconic dishes of Manipur. It's a spicy and tangy mashed dish made with boiled vegetables , fermented fish, and chili. It is often served with rice and is a staple food of the Manipuri people. Many dishes from Manipur are light yet flavorful, with an emphasis on fresh ingredients like fermented fish and local vegetables. Whether you're enjoying Eromba, Chamthong, or Chakhao, the cuisine of Manipur offers a rich and unique culinary experience.

Shopping in Manipur

The Phanek is a traditional Meitei skirt worn by women. It is made from handwoven fabric, often featuring bright colors and intricate patterns.Moirang Phee: This is a traditional shawl woven by the women of Moirang. It is made from fine silk or cotton, with intricate designs that have cultural significance. Moirang Phee is a symbol of Manipuri craftsmanship. Manipuri paintings often depict scenes from mythology, religion, and nature. Some of the prominent forms include Pahari and Pat painting styles.

JORHAT

Jorhat

Jorhat is a vibrant city located in the northeastern state of Assam, India. Known as the "Tea Capital of Assam. Tocklai Tea Research Institute, the oldest tea research institute in the world, is located in Jorhat. Located about 20 km from Jorhat, Majuli is the world's largest river island and is one of Assam’s most famous tourist destinations. The island is rich in Assamese culture, especially Vaishnavism, with several satra (monasteries) scattered around the island.

Kamakhya Temple

Kamakhya Temple is one of the most important and revered temples in India, dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya, a form of the divine feminine energy, Shakti. Situated on the Nilachal Hill in Guwahati, Assam.Kamakhya Temple is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas and holds immense significance in Hindu mythology and tantric practices. The temple's architecture is an outstanding example of Nagara style with a large dome-like structure known as the Shikhara.

Cole Park

Cole Park is a popular tourist destination located in Haflong, the only hill station of Assam, situated in the Dima Hasao district. Tezpur is a picturesque town known for its stunning landscapes, tea gardens, and historical sites. It is often referred to as the "Cultural Capital of Assam."

Local Cuisine

Assam's food is often characterized by mild spiciness, tangy flavors, and aromatic herbs and spices. Khar is one of the most iconic Assamese dishes. It is made using raw papaya, yellow lentils (toor dal), and a special ingredient called alkaline water extracted from the ashes of the burnt rice husks. Khar is a light yet flavorful dish often served with rice.A dish of fermented rice soaked in water, known as Pakhala, is a refreshing dish often served in the summer. It is typically paired with vegetables, fried fish, or meat.

Shopping in Jorhat

Shopping in Assam offers unique items like Assam tea, Muga silk, and handwoven textiles. Markets like Fancy Bazar feature bamboo products, traditional jewelry, and local handicrafts. Pithas (sweets), spices, and Bihu festival items make for great souvenirs.

CHERRAPENJI

Mawswai Cave

Mawsmai Cave is one of the most popular tourist attractions near Cherrapunji (Sohra) in Meghalaya. Located about 6 km from Cherrapunji, this limestone cave is famous for its stunning formations of stalactites and stalagmites.

Wei Sawdong

Wei Sawdong is a stunning, lesser-known waterfall located in the East Khasi Hills of Meghalaya, near Cherrapunji . Wei Sawdong is a three-tiered waterfall, with water cascading from a height of over 100 feet. The waterfall has a unique structure, where the water drops in three levels, creating a mesmerizing view and a serene atmosphere.

Sohra Village root bridge

The Living Root Bridges are one of the most remarkable natural wonders of Meghalaya, particularly the Khasi Hills region. These bridges are created by the indigenous Khasi people, using the roots of Ficus elastica . These bridges are "living" because the roots continue to grow and strengthen over time.

KOHIMA

Kohima War Cemetary

Kohima, the capital city of Nagaland, is located in the northeastern part of India, perched on the foothills of the Naga Hills.It provides an insight into the lifestyle of the indigenous people and is also a site for cultural festivals like the Hornbill Festival.

Dzokou Valley

Dzüko Valley is often referred to as the "Valley of Flowers".It's a popular destination for trekkers, nature enthusiasts, and adventure lovers due to its breathtaking views and relatively untouched natural beauty.Dzüko Valley is famous for the Dzüko lily, a rare and beautiful flower that blooms during the summer months, typically in June to September.The climate in Dzüko Valley varies with the seasons. Summers are mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 20°C.

Jotsoma

Jotsoma is another village near Kohima known for its rich cultural heritage and scenic views. It is home to the Angami Naga tribe and offers beautiful trekking trails, including those leading to Dzukou Valley. It is situated around 12 kilometers from the capital city of Kohima.Sekrenyi Festival, celebrated in February, is one of the major events in the village, marking the traditional new year for the Angami people. Visitors during this time can witness traditional dances, music, and rituals.

Local Cuisine

In Nagaland, try smoked pork with bamboo shoot, a signature dish, often paired with Zutho (rice beer) and fermented fish chutney. Enjoy a flavorful Naga chicken curry with rice and wild herb soup. For street food, don't miss grilled meats and fried bread with local chutneys.

Shopping in Kohima

Shopping in Nagaland offers unique local handicrafts like handwoven textiles, Naga shawls, and wooden carvings. You can also find traditional bamboo products, Naga jewelry, and fermented food items. Local markets, especially in Kohima and Dimapur, are great places to explore these authentic Naga souvenirs.

AIZWAL

Aizwal

Aizawl is situated on a series of hills, offering stunning views of the surrounding valleys and lush green landscapes. The natural beauty is one of its most defining features, with areas like Durtlang Hills and Reiek offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city and its surroundings.

Chapchur Kut

Celebrated in March.Chapchar Kut is the most significant festival of the Mizo people, marking the arrival of spring. It is traditionally a festival of harvest, celebrating the end of the hard labor of clearing the fields and preparing for the new season. People celebrate Chapchar Kut with traditional dances, music, and feasts. The festivities often feature Cheraw dance (the bamboo dance), where dancers weave in and out of bamboo poles held by others.

Phawngai Blue Mountain

Phawngpui, also known as Blue Mountain, is the highest peak in Mizoram, standing at 2,210 meters. Located in the Lushai Hills, it is a part of the Phawngpui National Park and offers stunning views, rich biodiversity, and trekking opportunities. The name "Blue Mountain" comes from the bluish hue it appears to have from a distance, especially during the monsoon season

Local Cuisine

The local food in Aizawl is mainly rice-based, with a variety of chutneys, meat, and fish dishes. Some popular local dishes include Bai (a Mizo vegetable dish), Sawhchiar (rice and meat), and Mizoram-style pork curry.Mizo cuisine is rich in flavors and largely centered around rice, pork, and local vegetables, with fermented fish and bamboo shoots playing key roles in many dishes. Vawksa Rep is a smoked pork dish. The pork is marinated with local spices, then smoked, giving it a distinct smoky flavor.

Shopping in Aizwal

Bazar Bungkawn is known for its traditional Mizo handicrafts, including handwoven Puan (traditional Mizo fabric), bamboo products, and other local crafts. The Mizo people have a long tradition of bamboo craftsmanship. You'll find other types of traditional Mizo clothing such as Mizo sarongs for men and dresses for women. Some sellers in the market offer antique items such as old coins, traditional tools, and artifacts.

PURI

Puri Jagannath Temple

The Jagannath Temple is known for its grand Kalinga-style architecture, with massive spires and intricate carvings. The temple complex is large and consists of several smaller shrines dedicated to other deities like Lord Balabhadra and Goddess Subhadra, who are the siblings of Lord Jagannath. The Rath Yatra, or the Chariot Festival, is one of the most famous events associated with the Jagannath Temple. During this festival, the idols of Lord Jagannath, Lord Balabhadra, and Goddess Subhadra are placed on enormous chariots and pulled through the streets of Puri by devotees.

Konark Temple

The Konark Sun Temple is designed in the shape of a giant chariot. This chariot, drawn by seven horses and featuring twelve intricately carved wheels, symbolizes the Sun God Surya's journey across the sky. Each of the twelve wheels represents a month, and the seven horses symbolize the days of the week. The temple is a masterpiece of Kalinga architecture.The temple’s central sanctum once housed a large idol of Lord Surya. The temple's alignment is such that the first rays of the sun fall on the idol of Surya in the sanctum at dawn, emphasizing its role as a Sun Temple.

Dhauli

The Dhauli Peace Pagoda is a symbol of peace and non-violence, constructed by the Japan Buddha Sangha and the Kalinga Nippon Buddha Sangha in 1972. Dhauli is historically famous for being the site of the Kalinga War in 261 BCE, a pivotal battle fought between the Mauryan Empire, led by Emperor Ashoka, and the kingdom of Kalinga. Rock Edicts of Ashoka are found on the Dhauli Hill, where Ashoka engraved his proclamations about peace, morality, and non-violence.

Odisha’s cuisine is known for its rich flavors, featuring dishes like Dalma , Pakhala Bhata, and Machha Besara Misti Dahi, or sweet curd, is a traditional sweet yogurt dish, typically served in clay pots. Patra is a savory snack made from colocasia leaves . The state is famous for its sweets, especially Rasgulla and Chhena Poda.
