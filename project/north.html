AYODHYA

Ram Janmabhoomi

The temple's history is intertwined with the Ramayana and holds great significance as the birthplace of Lord Rama. The temple is 360 feet long, 235 feet wide, and 161 feet tall. It's built without iron and is designed to last at least 1,000 years. The temple was inaugurated on January 22, 2024, by Prime Minister Narendra Modi. The temple's presiding deity is Balak Ram, crafted by Mysuru sculptor Arun Yogiraj.

Hanuman garhi

Hanuman Garhi is a revered 10th-century Hindu temple located in the heart of Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India. Dedicated to Lord Hanuman, it holds significant spiritual importance, especially for devotees visiting the Ram Janmabhoomi. Traditionally, pilgrims visit Hanuman Garhi before proceeding to the Ram Temple. The temple is designed in the shape of a fort, symbolizing its historical role in protecting Ayodhya.

Kanak Bhavan

Kanak Bhavan is a revered temple in Ayodhya, dedicated to Lord Ram and Sita. Known as the "Golden Palace," it features ornate architecture and intricate carvings. Believed to be gifted to Sita by Queen Kaushalya, it houses beautiful idols of the deities adorned with gold crowns. The serene ambiance attracts pilgrims and history enthusiasts alike.

Local Cuisine

Ayodhya offers a mix of traditional and local flavors, including Poori-Sabzi, Kachori, and Litti Chokha. Popular sweets like Peda and Jalebi are must-tries. Street food includes samosas and chaats, while many restaurants serve vegetarian North Indian thalis, reflecting the city’s spiritual and cultural essence. Savor kachoris, jalebis, and traditional sweets like peda and gujiya.

Shopping in Ayodhya

Shopping in Ayodhya offers a mix of spiritual and cultural delights. Popular items include religious souvenirs, brass idols, Ramayana-themed artifacts, handcrafted jewelry, and vibrant textiles. The bustling local markets also feature traditional sweets and herbal products. For pilgrims and tourists alike, Ayodhya's shopping experience captures the city's heritage and charm. Buy souvenirs like religious artifacts, brassware, and handicrafts from local markets.

VARANASI

Kashi Vishwanath Temple

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, is one of the most revered Hindu temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It stands as one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, symbolizing Shiva's supreme presence. The temple's origins trace back to ancient times, with the current structure built in 1780 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore. Over the centuries, it has faced multiple destructions and reconstructions, notably during the Mughal era. In 2021, the Kashi Vishwanath Dham Corridor was inaugurated.

Kashi Vishalakshi Devi Temple

The Kashi Vishalakshi Devi Temple in Varanasi is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, dedicated to Goddess Vishalakshi, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati. It is believed to mark the site where Sati’s earrings fell. Located near the Kashi Vishwanath Temple, it combines spiritual significance with architectural beauty. Devotees visit for blessings of prosperity and well-being, especially during Navratri and other Hindu festivals, making it a revered pilgrimage site.

Ghats

Varanasi’s ghats, located along the Ganges River, are spiritual hubs where pilgrims perform rituals and offer prayers. Famous ghats include Dashashwamedh Ghat, known for its evening Aarti, and Manikarnika Ghat, where cremations take place. Each ghat has its own significance, with some dedicated to bathing, rituals, and others to temples. The ghats are integral to the city’s spiritual identity, offering a peaceful, sacred atmosphere for reflection.

Ganga Aarti

The Ganga Aarti is a captivating evening ritual held at Dashashwamedh Ghat in Varanasi. Devotees gather to witness priests perform the ceremony, offering prayers and lighting oil lamps to honor the sacred Ganges River. The ceremony, accompanied by chants, bells, and incense, creates a mesmerizing spiritual experience, symbolizing devotion and gratitude towards the river.

Local Cuisine

Varanasi's local cuisine reflects its rich heritage. Must-tries include Kachori-Sabzi, Chaat, and Malaiyo, a frothy milk dessert unique to the city. Savor Banarasi Paan, the iconic betel leaf preparation, and enjoy sweet treats like Rabri and Jalebi. Street food like Tamatar Chaat and spicy snacks are popular among locals and visitors alike. Banarasi paan and Lassi are very famous delicacies that are a must try .

Shopping in Varanasi

Shopping in Varanasi is a treasure hunt through its bustling markets. The city is famous for Banarasi silk sarees, known for their intricate zari work and luxurious texture. Pick up wooden toys, handcrafted jewelry, and brass idols from local artisans. The markets also offer Gulabi Meenakari (pink enamel work), Rudraksha beads, and traditional musical instruments. Don't miss souvenirs like spiritual books, rosaries, and Banarasi paan masala. Popular shopping spots include Godowlia, Lanka, and Thatheri Bazaar, each bustling with unique finds.

LEH LADAKH

Pangong Tso

Pangong Tso is a stunning, high-altitude lake located in the Ladakh region of India, stretching across both India and China. Known for its mesmerizing changing colors, the lake spans 134 kilometers and is surrounded by rugged mountains. A popular tourist destination, it offers breathtaking views, and is known for its pristine beauty and serene, remote atmosphere.The beauty which awaits you there cannot be described in words. It has to be seen, felt and absorbed within yourself.

Leh Palace

The Leh Palace is a historic royal palace located in Leh, Ladakh. Built in the 17th century by King Sengge Namgyal, it overlooks the town and offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and the Indus Valley. The palace, an architectural marvel of Tibetan influence, houses artifacts and murals depicting the region's royal history. It stands as a reminder of Ladakh's rich cultural and historical heritage, attracting tourists for both its beauty and history.

Shanti stupa

The Shanti Stupa in Leh, Ladakh, is a white-domed Buddhist monument built to promote peace and commemorate 2500 years of Buddhism. Constructed by the Japanese Buddhist organization, it offers breathtaking views of Leh and the surrounding landscapes. The stupa houses relics of Buddha and is a symbol of harmony and tranquility. Pilgrims and tourists visit for its spiritual significance and the panoramic scenery, especially at sunrise and sunset.

Local Cuisine

Ladakhi cuisine is hearty and flavorful, designed for the region's cold climate. Popular dishes include Thukpa, a noodle soup with vegetables or meat, and Momo, steamed dumplings filled with vegetables or meat. Skyu, a traditional wheat dish, and Chhutagi, a pasta-based stew, are also favorites. Butter tea (Chai) is a common drink, often accompanied by Tsampa, roasted barley flour, for energy and warmth in the high-altitude environment.

Travel

Traveling to Ladakh offers an unforgettable adventure, with its stunning landscapes, monasteries, and unique culture. The region is accessible by road via the Manali-Leh Highway or Srinagar-Leh Highway, and by air through Leh Airport. Popular attractions include Pangong Tso, Nubra Valley, Magnetic Hill, and Khardung La, one of the highest motorable roads. Ladakh’s serene beauty, adventure activities, and Buddhist heritage make it a must-visit destination for travelers.

Shopping in Ladakh

Shopping in Ladakh offers unique handcrafted items reflecting the region’s culture. Popular purchases include Pashmina shawls, handwoven carpets, and traditional Tibetan jewelry. Thangka paintings (Buddhist scroll art), Kashmiri woolen products, and wooden artifacts are also sought-after. Ladakh is known for its local handicrafts, including leather goods and stone carvings. Markets like Leh Bazaar offer these authentic items, perfect for souvenirs and gifts.

AGRA

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, is an iconic symbol of love and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, the white marble mausoleum features exquisite Mughal architecture, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens. Completed in 1653, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors annually, celebrated for its stunning beauty and historical significance.

Fathepur Sikhri

Fatehpur Sikri is a historic city and UNESCO World Heritage Site located near Agra. Built by Mughal Emperor Akbar in the 16th century, it served as his capital for a brief period. The city showcases stunning Mughal architecture, with notable structures like Buland Darwaza, Jama Masjid, and Panch Mahal. Fatehpur Sikri is renowned for its blend of Persian, Indian, and Islamic architectural styles, making it a must-visit heritage site.

Akbhar tomb

Akbar's Tomb, located in Sikandra, Agra, is the final resting place of Mughal Emperor Akbar. Built by his son Jahangir, this majestic tomb blends Persian, Mughal, and Indian architectural styles. The tomb is set within a large, landscaped garden and features a grand entrance gate, intricate marble carvings, and beautiful motifs. It reflects Akbar's secular vision and is a significant historical and architectural landmark in Agra.

Local Cuisine

Agra offers a rich culinary heritage, influenced by Mughal and North Indian flavors. Must-try dishes include Petha, a sweet made from ash gourd, and Mughlai kebabs. Bedai (fried bread) with Aloo Sabzi is a popular breakfast dish. Chaat and Dahi Balla are street food favorites. Agra also serves delicious Tandoori chicken and Mutton Seekh Kebabs, along with lassi and sweetmeats for dessert.

Shopping in Agra

Shopping in Agra offers a variety of traditional handicrafts and souvenirs. The city is renowned for its Marble inlay work, especially Taj Mahal replicas. Popular items include Leather goods, such as shoes and bags, Chikan embroidery clothing, and Brassware. Agra is also famous for Petha, a local sweet, and Mughlai-style jewelry. Shopping hubs like Sadar Bazaar and Kinari Bazaar are great places to explore these unique products.

DELHI

India Gate

India Gate, located in New Delhi, is a war memorial built to honor the soldiers of the British Indian Army who died during World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. Completed in 1931, the structure stands 42 meters tall and is made of sandstone. The names of over 13,000 soldiers are inscribed on it. At night, the eternal flame of the Amar Jawan Jyoti honors fallen soldiers, making it a significant national landmark.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi. Built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-Din Aibak, it is the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at 73 meters. The tower is adorned with intricate carvings and inscriptions from the Quran. The Qutub Minar complex also houses other historical structures, including the Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque and the Iron Pillar, making it a significant symbol of Indo-Islamic architecture.

Lotus Pond

The Lotus Pond is a serene and picturesque spot in Delhi, located near the Lotus Temple. The pond is surrounded by lush greenery and features a stunning view of the temple's unique lotus-shaped structure. The pond adds to the tranquility and peaceful ambiance of the site, offering a calm space for visitors to relax and reflect. It complements the temple's architectural beauty, making it a popular tourist destination.

Local Cuisine

Delhi’s local cuisine is a vibrant mix of flavors, with influences from Mughal, Punjabi, and regional traditions. Popular dishes include Chaat, such as Pani Puri and Aloo Tikki, Chole Bhature, and Parathas. Kebabs, especially from Old Delhi, and Mughlai dishes like Biryani are iconic. Sweet treats like Jalebi, Kheer, and Kulfi are favorites. Delhi’s street food culture is also thriving with diverse options for every palate.

Shopping in Delhi

Delhi offers a rich shopping experience, from bustling markets to luxury malls. Chandni Chowk is famous for traditional items like sarees, jewelry, and spices. Connaught Place and Khan Market offer high-end brands and unique boutiques. Dilli Haat is a craft lover’s paradise with handicrafts from across India. For modern shopping, visit malls like Select Citywalk and DLF Mall of India, which feature international brands and entertainment options.
